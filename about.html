About SyTech

A Different Kind of Records Partner. Since 2000.

Most vendors sell public agencies another platform to license and maintain. Since 2000, SyTech has done the opposite. We build records management inside the cloud you already own, so you pay for the work, not someone else's software. Below is what makes that approach different, and how we got here.

2000

Founded in a garage.

Bryan Golden and two co-founders start SyTech with $20,000 — a document-scanning service, building agile from day one.

2002

Cloud, before it was the word.

SyTech builds its first native cloud records system — for Stockton Unified School District — years before the industry left on-premise servers.

2010s

Proven across California.

Expansion from single districts to county offices to statewide departments — hundreds of public agencies, one cloud engagement at a time.

2019

Connective tools ship.

ExpressLink releases — a lightweight watch-folder tool that feeds documents straight into the cloud. Connective tools, not more silos.

2021

50 million records.

The California Natural Resources Agency — 50M records digitized ahead of a 2021 building move. Likely the largest SharePoint Online deployment for a California public agency.

TODAY

The next 25 years.

25+ years in, and still modernizing public records the same way: inside the cloud you already own, one practical step at a time. Cloud, automation, and AI next.

For 25 years, we have watched agencies spend budget on annual software fees only to find their records buried inside platforms they don't control. Our approach is the opposite. Questions We Often Hear

Questions Worth Asking Before You Buy More Software.

Do we need to license another platform to modernize our records?

No. SyTech builds inside the cloud platforms your agency already owns — Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace. There is no new app to procure, no additional license to fund, no second login for your staff to manage. The capability is already in your tenant. We turn it on and configure it around how your team actually works.

We already have a document management system. Why change?

Most agencies are paying annual fees on systems that were built for a different era — closed platforms that don't connect to the cloud tools your staff use every day. We move your data out of those systems and into open cloud platforms, then build records management around the licenses you already fund. The result: one platform, lower recurring cost, full data portability.

Can we really build records management inside Microsoft 365 or Google?

Yes — and most agencies have no idea their current cloud subscription already supports it. Microsoft 365 and Google Workspace are not just productivity tools; they are extensible records and data platforms. The capability is there. What is usually missing is the implementation work, governance setup, and workflow configuration. That is the work we do.

What are best practices for our records and data going forward?

Move information into open, extensible cloud platforms. Avoid closed, hierarchical software that forces your processes to match its structure. Build retention, security, and workflow around your agency — not around a vendor's product roadmap. The cloud you already own can do all of this. Most agencies just need a partner who has done it across 4,579 cloud clients.

Cloud Solutions
A Cloud-First Practice — Since 2002

We Built Our First Cloud Deployment Before "Cloud" Was the Word for It.

In 2002, we built our first native cloud records management solution for Stockton Unified School District — years before the rest of the industry moved off on-premise servers. That early bet shaped everything that followed.

Since then, we have built thousands of cloud-based records and data services for public agencies across California, including Los Angeles County, the California Department of Child Support Services, the California Department of Natural Resources, and Yolo County District Attorney's Office. Open cloud was the right path then. It is the only path now.
